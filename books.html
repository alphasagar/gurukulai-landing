Just starting out? Read this first.

Before buying any book, understand the exam. UPSC has two stages — Prelims (objective MCQs, June every year) and Mains (written answers, October). Most beginners should focus on NCERTs first before any standard book. Don't buy everything at once — start with Class 6–10 NCERTs across History, Geography, and Polity.

Core Subjects & Standard Books

1. History

Must Read
Ancient & Medieval History
• Old NCERTs (Class VI–X, R.S. Sharma, Satish Chandra) Buy
• Tamil Nadu State Board History (Class 11 & 12) — concise & exam-oriented Buy
• Ancient India by R.S. Sharma Buy
• Medieval India by Satish Chandra (selectively) Buy
Modern History
• A Brief History of Modern India by Spectrum (Rajiv Ahir) — Bible for Prelims Buy
• India's Struggle for Independence by Bipan Chandra (selectively) Buy
Art & Culture
• Nitin Singhania's Indian Art & Culture (Prelims + Mains) Buy
• NCERT Fine Arts (Class 11) Buy

2. Geography

Must Read
• NCERTs: Class VI–XII (especially Class XI Physical Geography, Class XII India: Physical Environment) Buy
• Certificate Physical & Human Geography by G.C. Leong Buy
• Atlas: Orient Black Swan / Oxford Student Atlas Buy

3. Polity

Must Read
• Indian Polity by M. Laxmikanth — read cover to cover Buy
• NCERT Political Science (Class IX–XII) Buy
• Bare Act — for quick reference to Constitution articles

4. Economy

Must Read
• NCERT Economics (Class IX–XII) — especially Macroeconomics Class XII Buy
• Indian Economy by Ramesh Singh Buy
• Indian Economy by Sanjiv Verma (for clarity on basics) Buy
• Economic Survey (latest) — important for Prelims trends
• Union Budget (latest) — schemes, allocations, policies

5. Environment & Ecology

Important
• Shankar IAS Environment Book Buy
• NCERT Biology (Class XII) — Ecology & Environment chapters Buy
• Current affairs: Climate Change, COP, Biodiversity conventions

6. Science & Technology

Important
• NCERT Science (Class VI–X) for basics Buy
• NCERT Biology Class XI–XII (selectively)
Track Current Developments In:
• Space (ISRO, NASA), Defence technology, IT, AI, Robotics, Biotech

7. Current Affairs

Must Read
Newspapers (daily)
• The Hindu or Indian Express
Government Sources
• PIB (Press Information Bureau)
• PRS India (legislation tracking)
• Yojana & Kurukshetra (selective, important themes) Buy

8. CSAT (Paper-II)

Don't ignore
• Previous Year CSAT Papers — compulsory practice
• NCERT Maths (Class IX & X) for fundamentals Buy
• R.S. Aggarwal's Quantitative Aptitude (if weak in quant) Buy

Practice & Strategy

Practice Materials

• Previous Year Question Papers (PYQs) — minimum last 10 years
• Test Series — pick one institute and stick to it
• Daily MCQs — consistency over volume
• GurukulAI Prelims PYQ practice — free, unlimited, subject-wise

Preparation Strategy (Beginner to Prelims)

  1. Start with NCERTs (Class 6–10) across all subjects
  2. Then move to standard books — Laxmikanth, Spectrum, Leong
  3. Make short notes (one page per topic)
  4. Revise multiple times (3–4 revisions minimum)
  5. Solve PYQs — they reveal UPSC's exact pattern
  6. Take mock tests under real exam conditions

Mains is about answer writing — not just reading more books.
